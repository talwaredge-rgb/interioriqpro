01

A renovation quote has 200+ line items. Most homeowners review it once.

Quantities, unit rates, material specifications, exclusion clauses. Each one matters. Each one is an area where a homeowner without category knowledge is at a disadvantage. Not because the process is designed to deceive, but because this is a specialist document that rewards specialist reading.

02

Payment milestones should reflect work done, not work promised

The standard payment structure in Indian home interiors requires 30-40% upfront before work begins. Knowing what milestone structure to negotiate, and what to tie each payment to, is something most homeowners only learn after experiencing a delay or a dispute.

Tile alignment, cabinet gaps, paint coverage, waterproofing quality: these should all be verified before you sign off on completion. Having a structured checklist and knowing what pass or fail looks like for each trade is not confrontational. It is just thorough.

When you know what market rates are, what a fair specification looks like, and what questions to ask, conversations with your contractor become more productive. A good contractor welcomes an informed client. An independent review does not threaten a fair quote. It confirms it.

We reviewed your BoQ and identified 4 areas that warrant clarification before you sign. These are flags, not confirmed overcharges. We recommend raising each point with your contractor and requesting written clarification.
4 items flagged for your attention
01
False ceiling rate appears above current market range
The rate quoted for gypsum board false ceiling is at the higher end of what we typically see for this scope and city. Ask your contractor to break down what is included at this rate and confirm whether electrical channels and painting are included or separate.
Ask your contractor: "Can you confirm what is included in the false ceiling rate and provide a breakdown?"
02
Plywood grade not specified in kitchen cabinet line item
The BoQ lists "18mm plywood carcass" without specifying BWP, MR, or commercial grade. These have significantly different lifespans in a kitchen environment. Without a grade specification, the contract allows substitution to a lower grade after signing.
Ask your contractor: "Can you confirm the plywood grade as BWP (Boiling Water Proof) and add this to the specification?"
03
Waterproofing appears under two separate section headings
We noticed waterproofing listed in both the Civil Works section and the Bathroom section. This may be two separate scopes or the same work listed twice. Confirm with your contractor which lines cover which areas.
Ask your contractor: "Can you confirm whether the waterproofing in Civil Works and the bathroom waterproofing cover different areas or the same scope?"
04
Loft shutters, bathroom accessories, and balcony work not mentioned
These items are common to this scope but absent from the BoQ. They are not explicitly excluded either. Confirm in writing whether these are included, excluded, or to be priced separately.
Ask your contractor: "Are loft shutters, bathroom accessories, and balcony waterproofing included in this quote or will they be charged separately?"
Overall assessment
This BoQ is reasonably structured but has four areas that need written clarification before signing. The specification gaps on plywood and waterproofing are the most important to resolve. None of these require renegotiation. They require written confirmation of scope. Most contractors will agree to add specification details if asked directly.

Rate Padding

False ceiling: rate 61% above market

₹81,600
What the BoQ says

False ceiling (gypsum board, all rooms): 680 sq ft at ₹195/sq ft = ₹1,32,600

What the market says

Current contractor rate for gypsum board false ceiling in this city: ₹105-120/sq ft all-inclusive. Mid-point: ₹113/sq ft.

Our assessment

At ₹195/sq ft the rate is 61% above verified market rate for the same deliverable. Additionally, "electrical channels in false ceiling" is listed as a separate line item at ₹38/sq ft. This is standard included work at any rate above ₹140. Double-counted value: 680 x ₹38 = ₹25,840. Combined: ₹81,600.

Quantity Inflation

Living room + master bedroom flooring: 94 sq ft overbilled

₹42,300
What the BoQ says

Vitrified tile flooring: 580 sq ft at ₹450/sq ft = ₹2,61,000

What the floor plan says

Living room 18x14 ft = 252 sq ft. Master bedroom 14x12 ft = 168 sq ft. Less wardrobe footprint: 28 sq ft. Plus 8% wastage. Net: 423 sq ft.

Our assessment

BoQ quantity exceeds verified area by 157 sq ft. After fair wastage allowance, net overbill is 94 sq ft x ₹450 = ₹42,300. The contractor has the same floor plan the client shared with us.

Double Billing

Waterproofing billed under two separate heads

₹36,000

"Waterproofing treatment: bathrooms and utility" at ₹28,000 lump sum

Line item B (Bathroom section)

"Bathroom waterproofing: 3 coats Dr. Fixit" at ₹36,000 lump sum

Our assessment

Same scope described under two section headings. Contractor confirmed both cover the same work. ₹36,000 duplicate line to be removed entirely.
